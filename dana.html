Dana Rubin is an independent Bay Area illustrator. Her style can be described as joyful, whimsical, and free-form - inspired by nature and human expression. All artwork proceeds go to various nonprofits and charities.

The Brief:

Dana approached me about creating a gallery for her illustrations that would work as a launching pad to her e-commerce profiles where she sold her artwork. I was very impressed by her work and jumped on the project right away. I put together a web brief that would capture the requirements of her site and after some email exchanges we came up with a vision for her gallery.

Role:

Web and UI Design

Deliverables:

Visual identity system, UI elements, Visual Mockup, Web Development (on-going)

The challlenge and process:

We determined that the target audience would be art enthusiasts who shop at places such as etsy and studio6 or collect things on Pinterest. As the priority, this meant providing the ability to quickly and easily sift through multiple works in one go. It was also very important to create a site that would elevate her work and not compete with it visually.

We then worked together on a moodboard to capture her desired colors, aesthetic and visual feel. After some wireframe exploration, we landed on a grid style as the winning layout. From there I explored fonts and played with color hues, saturations and grays; as well as spacing and sizing of elements.
