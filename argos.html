ABOUT

Argos
Design

Argos Design is a boutique furniture manufacturing and design studio crafting custom pieces for individual and commercial clients across the U.S.

The Brief

The Argos Design studio founder, Cathy Dipietro, wanted to re-design her website and develop a brand identity that could be used on other mediums as well. During our initial chats, we both agreed on a new look and feel for the site: simple, modern, and confident - a reflection of Cathy’s personal style. We ironed out some other content and functional details, which I put into a digital brief and got to work.

Role:

Web and UI Design

Deliverables:

Visual Style Guide, Brand Strategy, Visual Mockup, and Web Development (on-going)

The Process

Building on top of an initial logo designed by another agency, I developed a broader branding identity for Argos, creating a new color palette and design guidelines to help her communicate a unified voice on other digital channels. With the brief in hand, I created a sitemap and some initial wireframes to resemble our new vision. Once she approved, I went on to create a high fidelity visual mockup.

Color and Type

The original moodboard had some cooler tones such as dark blue, green and brown. I played with the saturation and brightness of those colors to create a color palette with blue as its main color, a secondary dark brown, a complementary shade of tan and some grays. Her original logo used the ‘Trueno’ a modern and geometric type, so I explored a complementary font and landed on Open Sans because of its softer feel.

Overal the site’s design has come together pretty well and I have enjoyed helping the Argos Design brand grow. As my first project of 2020 I'm excited for the future opportunities this project will bring!
